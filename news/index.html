News

  • 8/26/2020 FDA approves Abbott BinaxNOW rapid antigen test for use in CLIA certified facilities or setting with CLIA certification waivers. This is a positive development, especially since the tests are affordable and don’t require separate electronic devices. However, they still require a prescription and they are encased in a cardboard cassette that significantly slows down manufacturing. This means they are unable to provide the levels of routine screening of asymptomatic individuals that we would need to reopen schools and offices.

  • 8/26/2020 The University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign announces it is performing rapid saliva testing on all students twice a week.

  • 8/16/2020 FDA approves SalivaDirect for PCR testing. This is a positive development, but it still requires processing in a central laboratory. By itself it will fall short of the widespread screening we would need to reopen schools and offices.

  • 8/4/2020 Six states join an interstate compact to order some rapid antigen tests. While this is a step in the right direction, regulations require that the tests be processed in laboratories, using devices that cannot be scaled as quickly as we need. If we want rapid testing on a scale that can cover schools and offices, we need to allow tests that can be processed anywhere and which do not require special devices.
